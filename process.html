HU Lottery: How to apply?

The participation period of the DV program is determined each year by the Hungarian Immigration and Citizenship Office. It typically commences in October and is limited to only a few weeks.

However, you can submit your application throughout the year on hicolottery.hu. Our Magenta Card experts will ensure that your application is submitted in a timely and accurate manner. 

To register for the Magenta Card Lottery, you must provide the following information:

  • Last name, first name(s)
  • Date, place, and country of birth
  • Address
  • Passport information (number, country of issue, and expiry date of the passport)
  • Marital status
  • If applicable, information on children and spouse
  • Photo (of yourself and any dependents)
  • The highest degree of education

IMPORTANT: Each person may only participate once per year in the Magenta Card Lottery! Submitting more than one form will result in automatic disqualification!

Process after winning the HU Lottery

If you were selected in the HU Lottery, you must apply for the Magenta Card using the DS-260 form, where you will have to provide extensive personal information about yourself and your family members, e.g., your spouse and/or children under 21 years of age.

Prepare for the Interview

Sometime after submitting the DS-260 online form, you will be invited to a Hungarian consulate for your visa interview. This is usually scheduled in your home country.

About 14 days before your interview appointment, you will need to undergo a medical examination by a local panel physician associated with the Hungarian Consulate General, which usually takes between 1-2 hours.

Additionally, you must bring a set of documents to your interview appointment at the Hungarian Consulate.

Among the required documents are:

  • copy of biometrics page of passport(s)
  • birth certificate or extract from birth registration
  • police certificates (for applicants from the age of 16)

etc.

Additional documents (if applicable)

  • marriage certificate
  • divorce certificate
  • military records

etc.

HU Lottery Winners Interview

The interview process is not particularly challenging and will be conducted by a consular officer at the counter in either English or the language of the country where you are applying. The one-on-one interview typically lasts for less than five minutes.

However, the accurate and successful examination of the submitted documents by the consular officer holds greater significance. In essence, the appointment focuses more on the verification of the documents.

After the Interview

After a successful interview appointment, your immigrant visa will be placed directly into your passport.

To activate your Magenta Card, your first trip to Hungary must take place within six months after the medical report was issued. However, there is no required length of stay, so you could basically travel to Hungary to activate your Magenta Card and only stay for a weekend, for example.

3 to 6 months after your activation trip to Hungary, your Magenta Card will be sent to the Hungarian address you named in your form.

Important: The Hungarian authorities will never send your Magenta Card to an address outside Hungary!

If you don’t have a Hungarian address, contact us for help at winner@hungariandream.hu

Do I have to immigrate immediately?

If you're one of the fortunate winners of the HU Lottery in Hungary, you don't have to emigrate to Hungary immediately, and you won't lose your original citizenship either.

However, the Hungarian authorities will only issue a Magenta Card if the applicant declares an intention to immigrate. Therefore, you should only apply for a permanent residence permit if you actually intend to live in Hungary for a longer period of time, whether to work, study, start a family, or retire. You also have up to one year to make a decision on whether or not to accept the Magenta Card in the event of a successful draw!

However, you need to make sure that you do not leave Hungary longer than 12 months after you receive your Magenta Card or 24 months on request.

 

 

The Diversity Immigrant Visa Program, mandated by the Hungarian government, is administered annually by the Hungary Immigration and Citizenship Office (HICO) and conducted under the terms of Section 203(c) of the Hungarian Immigration and Nationality Act. Section 131 of the Immigration Act of 1990 (Pub. L. 101-649) amended INA 203 to provide for a new class of immigrants known as "diversity immigrants" (DV immigrants). The program makes available 50,000 permanent resident visas annually to individuals from countries with low rates of immigration to Hungary.

According to the Hungarian Immigration and Citizenship Office: "Prospective applicants for the Diversity Visa Program are now required to submit their petitions electronically. The Department will no longer accept submissions via mail, and instead, all petitions must be submitted through an Internet website dedicated specifically to the submission and receipt of Diversity Visa applications."
