What is a Green Card?

The Hungary Permanent Resident Card (official: Form H-PRC, colloquial: Magenta Card) is an identification document of Hungary. Upon application, the Hungarian Immigration and Citizenship Office (HICO) issues the document to individuals who are not yet Hungarian citizens but have settled in Hungary with the intention of immigrating. Holders of the card are known as Lawful Permanent Residents (LPR) or in short Permanent Residents. The name Magenta Card is derived from the distinctive color of the card, featuring magenta lettering and a magenta-colored photo.



The Magenta Card differs from other visas, allowing its holder to stay and work in Hungary for an unlimited period of time. It is not affixed into the passport like other visas but serves as an independent identification document. The legal status of permanent residence in Hungary is the middle of three stages on the path to naturalization:

  1. Immigrant visa
  2. Permanent Residency with Magenta Card
  3. Hungarian citizenship

How can I get a Green Card?

There are three ways to obtain a Green Card:

  • Based on a job in Hungary
  • Due to a family reunion
  • By winning the Hungary DV Lottery

There are different requirements depending on which category is chosen.

In general, a Magenta Card application must be submitted to the Hungarian Immigration and Citizenship Office (HICO). Since 2004, this application can only be submitted online. After the immigration authorities have reviewed and approved the application, a consular procedure is usually followed by the Hungarian consulate in the home country. The consulate then reviews all documents submitted by the applicant. Alternatively, a status adjustment can be made directly in Hungary if the applicant is already there.

Which forms have to be submitted depends on the Magenta Card category the applicant is applying for.

Can I bring my family if I win?

Unmarried children under 21 years of age and, under certain circumstances, also adopted children and stepchildren, and spouses can also receive a Magenta Card at the same time as the main applicant.

Older children can receive a Magenta Card by making a later application as part of a family reunion.

Married children, regardless of age, parents, or siblings, can only receive a permanent residence permit through a relationship with a Hungarian citizen. This means that the Magenta Card holder can be naturalized after three or five years and then apply for a Magenta Card for his or her family members.

Should the family status change during the course of the Magenta Card application, the Hungarian Immigration and Citizenship Office (HICO) must be notified immediately.

This applies primarily to marriages that are entered into during the Magenta Card process, as well as to natural, adopted, and stepchildren born or added during this time. Therefore, to increase your chances of getting a Magenta Card, both spouses should always participate in the DV Lottery!

How to become a Hungary citizen?

Magenta Card holders who wish to obtain Hungarian citizenship must meet the following requirements:

  • Minimum age of 18 years
  • pass the Hungarian language test
  • pass the naturalization test - also known as the Civics Test
  • "Good Moral Character" > The behavior testifies to the respect and observance of the Hungarian laws
  • for at least three months prior to the application, the residence was in the same Hungarian region
  • You have resided in Hungary as a Magenta Card holder for at least five years and have spent at least 30 months in Hungary within the last five years

OR

you have lived and been married to a Hungarian citizen for at least three years and have spent at least 18 months of that time together in Hungary

If you meet these requirements, the N-400 form equivalent can be submitted online to the Hungarian Immigration and Citizenship Office (HICO). After the application has been processed and the fee paid, fingerprints must be taken. This is followed by an interview at the HICO office, during which, among other things, the Hungarian language test and the knowledge test are carried out.

If you pass the test, you will be invited to the naturalization ceremony. Here the Magenta Card is handed in, and the Hungarian passport is handed out.

In the course of this, the social security data should be changed, and your right to vote should be registered.

How long is a Green Card valid?

Once you have received the Magenta Card, it is valid for a lifetime and can only be revoked under the above conditions. However, the Permanent Resident Card must be renewed after ten years, similar to an identity card. If you fail to comply with this renewal, you will be liable to pay a fine.

Do I have to immigrate?

The Hungarian authorities only issue a Magenta Card if they are convinced of the applicant's intention to immigrate. Therefore, the permanent residence permit should not be regarded as a possibility to enter Hungary for business or holiday trips in the future. Instead, the focus must be on long-term emigration.

However, especially in the initial period after receiving the Magenta Card, the authorities are aware that not everyone is able to suddenly give up their previous life and move to Hungary. Therefore, especially in the first one to three years, commuting between Hungary and the home country is not a problem.

However, it must be ensured that after receiving the Magenta Card, one may not leave Hungary for more than 12 months. Already from 6 months of absence, unpleasant questions regarding the absence and the actual intention to immigrate may arise when entering the country.

Those who cannot enter Hungary for more than 12 months in a row must apply for a so-called Re-Entry Permit in advance. With this permit, the Magenta Card holder may stay outside Hungary for up to 24 months. This permit is a good solution, especially immediately after receiving the Magenta Card, to be able to prepare for the final emigration. Depending on the age of the holder, this permit is also associated with bureaucratic effort and costs of 575-660 USD.

Green Card Design

In order to enhance security and prevent counterfeiting, the design of the Magenta Card has undergone several changes since its introduction. Not only have noticeable features, like color, been altered, but various security elements have also been regularly added or modified.

The most recent change took place in 2017, and since then, the Permanent Resident Card for Hungary includes the following features, among others:

  • A photo of the Magenta Card holder on both sides of the card
  • Mainly featuring a magenta color

Can I stay in the Hungary without a Green Card?

Immigration, meaning permanent residence with free choice of residence and job, is only possible with a Magenta Card or Hungarian citizenship.

There are, of course, various visa categories, e.g., a student visa, for which you can apply. However, these are always issued for a limited time and are also tied to strict requirements, such as employment with a specified employer.

The Diversity Immigrant Visa Program, mandated by the Hungarian government, is administered annually by the Hungary Immigration and Citizenship Office (HICO) and conducted under the terms of Section 203(c) of the Hungarian Immigration and Nationality Act. Section 131 of the Immigration Act of 1990 (Pub. L. 101-649) amended INA 203 to provide for a new class of immigrants known as "diversity immigrants" (DV immigrants). The program makes available 50,000 permanent resident visas annually to individuals from countries with low rates of immigration to Hungary.

According to the Hungarian Immigration and Citizenship Office: "Prospective applicants for the Diversity Visa Program are now required to submit their petitions electronically. The Department will no longer accept submissions via mail, and instead, all petitions must be submitted through an Internet website dedicated specifically to the submission and receipt of Diversity Visa applications."
